# Book Spec and Offer Stack

Recommended build for the book and offer stack for *The Future of AI Is Dead*.

---

## Recommended Book Spec

For a CEO/business-entrepreneur AI book, the best-performing structure is:

- **40,000–55,000 words**
- **10 chapters**
- **180–230 pages**
- **About 4,000–5,500 words per chapter**
- **1 short intro and 1 concise closing section**

That keeps it authoritative without feeling heavy, and it matches common business/nonfiction length guidance while staying easy for busy readers to finish.

---

## Best Chapter Model

A strong chapter count for engagement is **8–12 chapters**, with **10 being the safest target** for this kind of practical business title.

A 10-chapter structure gives you room to cover AI fundamentals, hype vs reality, ROI, risks, implementation, governance, team adoption, and future strategy — without bloating the book.

---

## Pricing Recommendation

For direct sales through your own site:

| Price Point | Use |
|---|---|
| **$9.99** | Standard / regular price |
| **$7.99** | Launch offer price |

That price point is high enough to signal quality for a professional audience, but still low enough to feel "painless." Premium business ebooks commonly live in the $9.99 range for direct or platform sales.

### Why Not Cheaper?

If you go too low, the book can start to feel like a low-value lead magnet rather than a serious executive resource.

Because the funnel includes free chapter previews, upsells, reseller rights, and software offers, the book should act as a **credible front-end asset** that qualifies buyers for the higher-ticket stack rather than being the main profit engine.

---

## Best Discount Structure

| Tier | Price | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Regular price | **$9.99** | Standard listing |
| Launch price | **$7.99** | Limited-time launch window |
| Promo fallback | **$6.99** | Exit-intent popup (cursor moves to leave page) |

At this level, a small dollar discount often feels clearer than a percentage discount, and limited-time pricing increases action without destroying perceived value.

---

## Funnel Positioning

The book is the **"first yes"** — the entry point into the offer ladder.

Recommended funnel ladder:

```
Book ($9.99)
  └─ Order Bump (+$9)
       └─ Upsell 1 ($29)
            └─ Upsell 2 ($79)
                 └─ Upsell 3 ($149)
                      └─ Membership ($19/month)
```

This is consistent with modern book-funnel patterns that use a low-cost entry product to move buyers into bigger offers.

## Practical Recommendation

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Book length | ~45,000–50,000 words |
| Standard price | $9.99 |
| Launch promo | $7.99 |
| Exit-intent OTO (click-off popup) | $6.99 |
| Last-chance fallback | $5.99 |
| Profit layer | Upsells (keep these as the real revenue engine) |

---

## Summary

The book acts as a credible, low-friction front-end asset at **$9.99 ($7.99 launch)**. It qualifies buyers and moves them into the higher-ticket upsell stack. Discounts are dollar-denominated and time-limited. Exit-intent popups capture abandoning visitors at $6.99 → $5.99 last chance. Upsells are where the real profit lives.
